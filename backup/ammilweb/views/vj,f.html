US Visitor Visa Interview Questions & Answers

Chinenye497 2d

Below are commonly asked questions during the USA visitor visa interview. 

Q: Why are you going to the USA?

A: Tell them you are going on a visit.

Q: Have you been to the USA before?

A: Give the true reply If you have been there before, say Yes, and if not, say No.

Q: Do you have relatives in the US? 

A: Give if true reply. If you have relatives say Yes otherwise say No.

Q: Who will sponsor your visit?.

A: Give a honest reply

Q: Where will you stay in the USA?

A: Give the address of where you will stay. 

Q: What does your host do for a living?

A: Tell them what he or she does for a living. If your host is working with a company, tell them the name of the company. 

Q: How much does your host earn?

A: Give a honest reply.

Note: Carry documents to show as evidence.

Q: How long has your host been in the USA?

A: Tell them the number of years your host has lived in the US.

Q: When do you plan to travel?

A: Tell them the month you plan to travel 

Q: Why would you want to travel at that time?

A: Give your reasons.

Q: What are you going to do in the US?

A: Give a honest reply.

Q: What is the purpose of your trip?

A: Tell them the purpose of your visit.

Q: Have you booked your tickets?

A: Say Yes if you have and No if you haven’t.

Q: Do you have return air tickets, medical insurance, etc.

A: Explain whether or not you have a return ticket and medical insurance.

Have you traveled outside your home country before?

A: Give a honest answer Yes or No.

Q: Is this your first visit to the US?

A: Give a honest reply.

Q: What places do you want to visit?

A: Give names of interesting tourist attractions places that you know.

Q: How long will you stay in the US?

A: Tell them the number of months, for example, six months.

If you are staying for longer than six months, you may be asked;

Q: Why do you want to stay for so long?

A: Give a strong reason why you would like to stay for so long.

Q: What is the guarantee that you will come back?

A: Give strong reasons why you will return to your country. Showing them evidence of the ties you have in your country. If you have a job or a business, mention it. 

Note: Have documents to back up your claims.

Q: Where do you stay in your country?

A: Tell them where you stay.

Q: What do you do for a living?

A: Give the correct answer. If you are retired, say so.

Q: What is your job profile?

A: Give job details, responsibilities, etc.

Q: What is your annual income?

A: Give honest answers and details.

Note: Show documents to support your answers.

Q: If you are working, you may be asked: Did you take leave?

A: Give a true answer. If you took leave, show leave certificate.

Q: Your income is not enough to support international travel. What do you plan to do?

A: Tell them your host is sponsoring your visit if that is the case.

Q: If you run a business, you may be asked: Who will look after your business in your absence?

A: Explain to them the arrangements you have made so far. If you have a partner or a manager who is capable of running the business in your absence, tell them about it.

Note: Ensure that you tell the truth because if they find out that you are lying, you will be denied Visa.
